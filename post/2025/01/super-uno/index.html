Super UNO - English Patch

 January 23, 2025

Super UNO (スーパーUNO) is a Super Famicom game released on November 12, 1993 in Japan.

It’s a console version of the card game Uno for 1 or 2 players. You can play against CPUs for a total of 6 players.

There’s a regular mode, a team mode, as well as a board game mode.

The game includes the Uno rules.

There are two scoring variants and a “house rule” option where you can play +2 and +4 on top of each others.

Item


The item option adds a memory mini-game for the winner of a game. If you find at least one pair, you get an item to use at the start of the next game.

See the item list below (marked Uno).

. .

Board Game


In the board game mode, you compete against 3 opponents. You move around a board where the spaces each have different effects (bonus points, movement, items).

Along the way, you acquire items that can be used on the board to mess with other players, or at the start of Uno games to help you win.

There are different 5 boards. The first 4 boards each have 4 castles where you play a Uno game to rescue someone. Beating the 4th castle of each board triggers the end.

The 5th board only has one castle where the final opponent is.

There’s a password system to save between each board.

Retro-bit's Shockman Zero translation is plagiarized

 February 8, 2025


What is this about?


On January 29 2023, an unofficial English translation of the Super Nintendo game Shockman Zero was released on romhacking.net by Svambo, Anonymousse and Nokia3310.

More than a year later, between June 24, 2024 and July 29, 2024, retro-bit.com opened pre-orders for the cartridge release of the same game, in English as well. (MSRP: $54.99 USD / €64.99 EUR)

https://retro-bit.com/shockman-zero/

The FAQ that you can find at the bottom of the page (https://retro-bit.com/shockman-zero/) states:

I highly suspect, and I will show evidence why, that their version is a rip-off of the Svambo English patch from 2023.


Evidence A: the font


The original Japanese game has an 8x8 latin font for the menus/ui. There’s also a partial font for the words “START”, “STAGE CLEAR” and “GAME OVER”.

But all the dialogs are using a 16x16 font in kanas and kanjis with a few exceptions like the “B” in BB団.

Which is why Svambo had to create a custom 8x16 latin font for the dialogs. I looked at both roms and this font is not in the original Japanese rom.

When looking at the retro-bit Press Kit release or their own video of the game: https://youtu.be/YhrawWNKsM4, it is clear that the font they used is identical to svambo’s.

Another example:

The Japanese き〜！(ki~) is an onomatopea for a long hysterical scream. In manga, that would translated by “screech”.

retro-bit release Svambo patch

Not only do both translations keep that “ki” as if it were Japanese but they share the same odd formatting “…kee…!”


I could go on, but I can’t see more of the retro-bit release than what people have uploaded on YouTube and I think I’ve already made my point anyway.


Who did the retro-bit translation?


According to the manual:

Roberto Mérida Fernandez who goes by Rod Merida online, is active on romhacking.net as you can see on his profile:

https://www.romhacking.net/community/5844/

So it is hard to believe that he was not aware of the existence of Svambo’s patch.

Manual image from: Archades Games - Shockman Zero Unboxing


My opinion


Between the identical font and the many similarities between the scripts, I refuse to believe that Svambo’s patch was not heavily used for this translation.

Not only that, but the changes made were either irrelevant or worse than before.

All things considered, this would not be a big deal if it weren’t for the 9000 copies produced with a hefty price tag of $55 / €65.

Plagiarism aside, for the price of a AAA game, you should expect a modicum of professional quality. That’s clearly not there.
