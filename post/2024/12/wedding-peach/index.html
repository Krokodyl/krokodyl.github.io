Wedding Peach - English Patch

 December 28, 2024

Wedding Peach (ウェディングピーチ) is a Super Famicom game released on September 29, 1995 in Japan.

It is based on the anime of the same name: Wedding Peach (愛天使伝説ウェディングピーチ).

It’s a party game for 1 to 3 players. It contains 8 different mini-games.

The story is a tournament over 3 days of the 8 mini-games and is based on a specific episode (see anime section).

I am aware of the existing translation patch by Chaotic Translations released in 1998. I didn’t use it for my translation, I worked from scratch.

Ending


In the story mode, after the 3rd day, there’s a final game. It’s a battle against Igneous (イグニス igunisu) and Blackie.

You have to dodge attacks while shooting them. It’s not shown but everyone starts with 100 pts. You get 10 pts if you hit Blackie, 20 pts for Igneous. You lose 5 pts if the fire wave hits you.

Whoever deals the most damage gets a bonus: 100, 200 or 300 pts. (respectively for 4/6/8 games per day)

Igneous has a final dialogue which changes if he escaped or not.

Depending on who got the most points, there’s a short cutscene:

Momoko and Yousuke Yuri and Yanagiba Hinagiku and Takuro

The final scene is always the same. It’s just like the ending of the anime episode.


The anime


If you’re note familiar with the anime: here’s a good and quick overview.

The game is loosely based on episode 24 that you can watch here.

or there:

Open Video Player

The story mode reuses the main story line of that episode and even uses lines from it verbatim.

The two main differences are that the game doesn’t mention Takuro being possessed by Igneous, and Blackie doesn’t transform into a human-sized devil.

Overall, the game transcribes the visual identity of the anime pretty well, it’s a shame that the minigames are not great (at least as a single player).

Voices


When the girl transforms into their Love Angel counterpart, they have a call (a common magical girl trope).
The game uses samples of the actual voice actors of the anime. It’s probably why they are credited at the end of the game.

Sample Audio

Translation


The character that acts as the game host is Jama-P.

In the original version, he speaks with a kind of lisp and ends all phrases with ピー (pi).

For example, instead of ください (kudasai) he says くだちゃいピー (kudachaipi), instead of です (desu) he says でちゅピー (dechupi).

In this translation, I wrote him as speaking normally. I didn’t want to have him say “pee” at the end of every sentence and confuse anyone who has no reference to the Japanese version. I wonder how the English dub of the anime handled that (couldn’t find any video of it).

Retro-bit's Shockman Zero translation is plagiarized

 February 8, 2025


What is this about?


On January 29 2023, an unofficial English translation of the Super Nintendo game Shockman Zero was released on romhacking.net by Svambo, Anonymousse and Nokia3310.

More than a year later, between June 24, 2024 and July 29, 2024, retro-bit.com opened pre-orders for the cartridge release of the same game, in English as well. (MSRP: $54.99 USD / €64.99 EUR)

https://retro-bit.com/shockman-zero/

The FAQ that you can find at the bottom of the page (https://retro-bit.com/shockman-zero/) states:

I highly suspect, and I will show evidence why, that their version is a rip-off of the Svambo English patch from 2023.


Evidence A: the font


The original Japanese game has an 8x8 latin font for the menus/ui. There’s also a partial font for the words “START”, “STAGE CLEAR” and “GAME OVER”.

But all the dialogs are using a 16x16 font in kanas and kanjis with a few exceptions like the “B” in BB団.

Which is why Svambo had to create a custom 8x16 latin font for the dialogs. I looked at both roms and this font is not in the original Japanese rom.

When looking at the retro-bit Press Kit release or their own video of the game: https://youtu.be/YhrawWNKsM4, it is clear that the font they used is identical to svambo’s.

The line よくおぼえておくわ means “I’ll remember this well”. In the Svambo translation, the translator took the creative liberty to translate it as “I’ll put you on my list!”.

Another example:

The Japanese き〜！(ki~) is an onomatopea for a long hysterical scream. In manga, that would translated by “screech”.

retro-bit release Svambo patch

Not only do both translations keep that “ki” as if it were Japanese but they share the same odd formatting “…kee…!”


I could go on, but I can’t see more of the retro-bit release than what people have uploaded on YouTube and I think I’ve already made my point anyway.


Who did the retro-bit translation?


According to the manual:

Roberto Mérida Fernandez who goes by Rod Merida online, is active on romhacking.net as you can see on his profile:

https://www.romhacking.net/community/5844/

So it is hard to believe that he was not aware of the existence of Svambo’s patch.

Manual image from: Archades Games - Shockman Zero Unboxing


My opinion


Between the identical font and the many similarities between the scripts, I refuse to believe that Svambo’s patch was not heavily used for this translation.

Not only that, but the changes made were either irrelevant or worse than before.

All things considered, this would not be a big deal if it weren’t for the 9000 copies produced with a hefty price tag of $55 / €65.

Plagiarism aside, for the price of a AAA game, you should expect a modicum of professional quality. That’s clearly not there.
